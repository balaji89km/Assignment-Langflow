<div class="section">
    <h2>Improvement Recommendations</h2>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Rewrite the title and description.</strong> Use the standard “As a [role], I want [capability] so that [benefit]” format. Identify the real actor (e.g., “clinical manager”, “reporting analyst”).</li>
        <li><strong>Define concrete acceptance criteria.</strong> List measurable conditions (e.g., “Report can be generated for a selected date range”, “Data export complies with HL7‑CDA”, “Access is audited per HIPAA”).</li>
        <li><strong>Specify pre‑conditions and dependencies.</strong> Indicate required data sources, user permissions, or upstream services.</li>
        <li><strong>Articulate business value.</strong> Explain how the report improves patient safety, regulatory compliance, or operational efficiency (e.g., “enables quarterly audit of adverse events”).</li>
        <li><strong>Make the story testable.</strong> From the acceptance criteria, derive at least three test scenarios (positive, negative, security). Include performance expectations if the report is large.</li>
        <li><strong>Assess technical constraints.</strong> Mention integration points (EHR, data warehouse), expected data volume, and any regulatory encryption requirements.</li>
        <li><strong>Include non‑functional requirements.</strong> Security (HIPAA‑compliant transmission), availability (report generation < 5 seconds), and audit logging.</li>
    </ul>
</div>
